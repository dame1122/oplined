The role

What Is an AI Operator?

They sit between strategy and execution. They're not a developer who needs someone to tell them what to build. They're not a consultant who diagnoses and walks away. They do both. Diagnosis to deployment. One person.

  • Audit internal workflows and identify automation opportunities
  • Build AI agents, automations, and RAG systems in production
  • Integrate with existing CRMs, ERPs, and internal tools
  • Replace annual agency and contractor spend with one hire
  • Train internal teams to maintain and scale what they build

FAQ

Questions.

for operators
Who is this for? +

Operators who have shipped AI systems in production. Not students. Not hobbyists. Not prompt engineers. If you've built and deployed real systems that run in a business, apply.

What do you look for? +

Production experience with tools like Claude Code, Cursor, Make, n8n, or equivalent. You understand business operations. You diagnose before you build. You've worked directly with business owners, not only engineering teams.

What's the compensation? +

$150K+ base. Select roles include equity. Compensation is determined by the hiring company.

How selective is this? +

Placement rate is under 3%. We review every application individually. The live build assessment is the deciding factor, not your resume.

Is this remote or in-person? +

Depends on the company. Preferences are part of the matching process. Roughly 60% of current roles are hybrid or in-person.

for companies
Who is this for? +

Companies doing $5M-$50M in revenue with 10+ employees. You have workflow bottlenecks, you've tried an agency or consultant, and you need someone full time who owns AI across your business.

How does it work commercially? +

Companies pay a network fee on successful hires. Operators pay nothing. No retainers. No upfront commitments.

What if it's not the right fit? +

30-day evaluation window. If it's not right, we rematch at no additional cost.

How is this different from a recruiter? +

Recruiters match resumes to job descriptions. The AI operator role doesn't exist on traditional hiring platforms. We vet operators through live builds and match them to your actual business.

What does an operator do inside my company? +

They audit your workflows, identify the highest-value automation opportunities, and build the systems. They integrate with your existing tools. They train your team. They own AI so you don't have to.

for founders
What's the structure? +

Varies by deal. We match operators who want co-founder dynamics. Equity splits are negotiated between both parties. Oplined facilitates the introduction and vetting.

Do I need a product already? +

No. You need domain expertise, distribution, or a validated concept. The operator brings the technical execution.

What industries? +

If there's a workflow problem and a market, there's a fit.
